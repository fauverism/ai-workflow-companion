# Outreach Templates

Short, specific, no pitch voice. Two variants: internal (your org) and external (peers/teams). Personalize the [bracketed] line — it's the whole message.

---

## Internal — to an eng lead or team

Subject / opener: **The gap between "we have Claude Code" and "we use it well"**

Hey [name] — quick one.

We've got [Claude Code] licenses across the team, but from what I can see, usage is mostly [autocomplete-level / individual and ad-hoc — adjust to what you've actually observed].

I built a small internal tool that turns the documented best-practice workflow into daily habits: a Crawl/Walk/Run ladder of observable milestones, plus copy-paste slash commands and hooks we can check into our repos today. No backend, no procurement — it's a static page.

Could I get 15 minutes to walk you through it with [your team / one pilot team]? If the ladder doesn't surface anything useful about where we're stalled, that's a cheap finding too.

Hi [name] — [one specific line about why them: their post about AI workflow adoption, their team's stack, a talk they gave].

---

**Usage notes**

- Never send without replacing the [bracketed] personalization — a generic version of this message is worse than no message.
- The internal variant's close ("cheap finding") is deliberate: it gives the recipient a win even if they're skeptical of the tool.
- Don't stack both credibility claims (Boris + 25 years) into one sentence when trimming — pick whichever the recipient will weight more.
